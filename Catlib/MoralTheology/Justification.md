# CCC §1987–1993: Justification by Grace

## The Catechism claim

Grace justifies us — cleanses from sin and communicates God's
righteousness (§1987). Justification establishes cooperation between
God's grace and man's freedom (§1993). It is conferred in Baptism
(§1992).

## What we found

### The axiom set IS the denomination

The most important structural result in the entire project. The
Catholic-Protestant disagreement on justification can be formalized
as a difference in axiom sets:

| Axis | Catholic | Protestant (Lutheran) |
|------|----------|----------------------|
| Grace model | Transformative (changes you) | Forensic (declares you righteous) |
| Human role | Cooperation (enabled by grace) | Reception only (faith alone) |
| Instrument | Baptism (sacramental) | Faith alone |
| Timing | Process (ongoing) | Event (single moment) |

The Protestant position is obtained by replacing/dropping all four
Catholic axioms. Change the axioms, change the denomination.

### Hidden assumptions

1. **Grace is transformative** — actually changes the person, not just
   their legal status (the claim Luther rejected)
2. **Human cooperation is real** — connects to the §2001 bootstrapping
   problem; cooperation is a gift but still genuine
3. **Baptism confers justification** — ties salvation to the Church
4. **Justification is a process** — not a single moment
