# CCC §302–311: Divine Providence and Free Will

## The Catechism claim

God guides all creation (§302), knows all future events including free
ones (§302), operates "in and through" all creature actions (§308), but
is "in no way" the cause of moral evil (§311). Creatures are genuine
causes with real freedom (§306, §311).

## What we found

### The causation asymmetry

God's relationship to causation changes based on moral character:
- Good acts: God "operates in and through" them
- Evil acts: God merely "permits" them

This asymmetry requires evil to be a privation (absence of good) rather
than a positive reality — otherwise God would need to cause it. The
privation theory of evil is doing invisible work.

### Hidden assumptions

1. **Two-tier causation** — primary and secondary causes don't compete
2. **Granted causation** — creaturely causation is real but derived
3. **Foreknowledge-freedom compatibility** — asserted, no mechanism
4. **Good/evil causation asymmetry** — God's causal role differs by
   moral character of the effect
5. **Permission ≠ causation** — knowing, having power, and permitting
   is different from causing
6. **Freedom > evil** — the value of freedom justifies permitting evil

### Assessment

**Tier 3** — Six major axioms in ten paragraphs. The causation asymmetry
between good and evil was the most surprising finding.
