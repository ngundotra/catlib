sinlessness_implies_bodily_integrity

axiom — Catlib/Creed/MarianDogma.lean:494

Catholic

⁂

Lean Declaration

axiom sinlessness_implies_bodily_integrity :
  ∀ (p : HumanPerson),
    ¬hasOriginalSin p →
    ¬committedPersonalSin p →
    ¬bodySubjectToCorruption p
❅

Why This Axiom?

AXIOM: sinlessness_implies_bodily_integrity — If a person never sinned (neither original nor personal sin), their body is not subject to the corruption that results from sin. (the contrapositive: no sin → no corruption-from-sin); CCC §966, §974. but stated as a separate axiom because the reasoning involves an additional step: not just "no sin → no death" but "no sin → bodily integrity preserved for assumption into glory." integrity is accepted primarily in Catholic theology.

Source: Theological reasoning from death_is_consequence_of_sin
